Hello people! This is my brand new website, and a brand new blog to go with it.

I'm starting this blog, because due to obvious reasons, I'll have some more time home alone. I'll probably be writing about my work and life as a web developer, who happens to also be interested about user experience, business, leadership etc. I'm not gonna be limiting myself too much though, so we'll see.

On the latest [Quartzy newsletter](https://qz.com/emails/quartzy/1817363/?utm_source=email&utm_medium=quartzy) Tonly Lin encouraged everybody to _find something to make you happy at home_, which for me meant starting this blog. I don't know if this will actually make me happy in the long run, but this is definitely something I've always meant to do.

Starting a blog for me meant that I picked one or two new exciting web technologies and ways of development, created a new website, deployed it and then started to write this blog that gets published on it. That may be quite a lot of useless effort, but that's what gets me excited, so be it.

And that's basically the anatomy of a good side project, at least for me:
- It's not a short-lived idea, but something you've wanted to do for some time
- You can and will learn from it
- It brings joy
- There are things, important or otherwise, that you really can and want to obsess over
- It doesn't feel like work
- You are embarassed to show it to others

Adapting to a changing world around us, be it a new Javascript framework outbreak or development of a new virus, is what we us developers are proficient at. So take a deep breath and deal with it.
