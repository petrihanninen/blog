There are quite a few things that one should consider when changing jobs. Here I'll try to think through some of those, and why they matter to me or not.

## Salary / Monetary Compensation

I think that bigger salary is a worthwhile pursuit for a few reasons. Firstly, it is a metric of how much you provide value to your employer — bigger salary means that your actions are actually worth more to the business. Also, more money obviously lets you have more freedom.

Other types of monetary compensations are a bit harder to process: stock options could or could not be worth a lot, though they could also be worth nothing. So, I wouldn't go for any significant pay decrease in hopes of a big pay day. Then again, if ESO (Employee Stock Options) are an addition to otherwise static salary, that could be great motivational boost.

## Devices

If I don't get the devices of my choosing necessary for my work, I find it a bit lacking compared to current industry standards. Especially, as I don't have any proper laptop or phone as I've used provided stuff for quite a while. Not getting devices from my employer would mean that I'd have to spend at least 3000 €, or more than one month's net income, to purchase those.

## Perks

I think perks are a good indicator of the company culture – if an employer provides budget and time for self-development, values clearly show through. Also, if perks include interesting sports-related stuff, there are probably some people that I could join for a run or invite climbing. If on the other hand company provides limitless beer in fridges, there are probably some good times to be had at the office after hours, which I at this point in life still enjoy.
