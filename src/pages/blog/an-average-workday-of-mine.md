Shortly after my summer vacation, I was tasked with writing a few words about an "average" day of mine, whatever that means. Now the (story is out)[https://duunitori.fi/tyoelama/duunitori-ohjelmistokehitys-tyopaiva/] (in Finnish). Here's a short-ish summary in English as well. I'll probably write more about this subject, especially reasons and thoughts behind the tasks, later.

## An Average Day at Work

### 8.30
To start things off, mornings tend to be hard. That's why I start it slow and steady, planning what I wish to accomplish today. This is especially important, as at the moment roughly a third of my time is spent actually doing development, and the rest I spend on various planning, managing, coaching tasks.

### 10.00
I was tasked to find some data related to how the amount of job ads on specific industries has changed due to the recent events. Making queries was definitely not my strong suit, so I had to do quite a bit of googling and testing. After that I wrote myself (a blog post)[/blog/calculating-stuff-with-sql/] so that I don't have to rely so much on Stackoverflow the next time.

### 11.15
Time to grab some lunch. Today is a good day for some pad thai.

### 12.00
Improving our GTM and analytics tracking, to make sure our ad money is spent as efficiently as possible.

### 13.00
Planning stuff related to our project of improving our digital sales funnels. I'm in charge of managing this project as a whole, which is definitely intimidating but exciting.

### 15.30
Last task of the day is fixing a bug on our advertisement dashboard. After some hand-wavy work, a straight forward bugfix project is quite refreshing.

### 16.30
To end the day, I try to reflect on the day – what went well and what things could do use some sparring with a co-worker. Then it's time to shut down the computer and head bouldering.
