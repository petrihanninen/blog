My team and I were just deciding on our Objectives and Key Results (More about OKR model <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OKR">here</a>) for Q3/2020, and here are a few thoughts on why setting good, measurable key results is difficult, but why that's an integral job for any successful team.

## Finding Focus

A huge issue pretty much everywhere is lack of focus: No defined goals or even worse too many goals is a guaranteed way for lack of motivation and direction. Instead, if you set 2-4 goals to your company / team / self for a perioid of time, you can look at every task and every decision and ask "Is this taking me towards those?"

In general, a good way to prioritise tasks if to ask "Does this take me towards my goals?". If yes, it's a high priority task. If not, ask "Is this something actually urgent?" If yes, it's a high priority task. If not, just don't do it. We're going for a <a href="https://sivers.org/hellyeah">"If it's not a 'Hell Yeah!' it's a no"</a> -mindset. Oh, and I'd define urgent as "not fixing will prevent many people from giving me/us money" or "not fixing will cost me/us at least an hour for every minute of work put in".

## Don't aim too low

If you always hit your every goal, your aim has been set way too low. In fact, you should probably fail in 20 - 30 % of your goals. This makes sure that your goals actually give you a clear direction. If my goal is to get 10 more readers every month, I don't have to do any decisions, as probably the traffic by random bots will increase that much. Then the goal could as well not exist. If on the other hand I set my goal to get 1000 more readers every month, I actually need to take that goal into account when both writing and promoting this blog. In this scenario I probably won't get to 1000, but I could well get to 700, and that's a win.

This doesn't mean that you should aim for 70 % completion, though, but for at least 100, maybe 110 %. If I aim for 1000, I might get to 700. If, on the other hand, I aim for 700, I'll might get to 450. That could be cool and all, but nowhere near of the potential gains I could've had.

## Don't aim too high

It's also important to realise that a good goal is achievable. If I set my goal to 10k new readers, it's impossible (nothing is impossible and blah blah, we'll go with 10k for illustrative purposes). With an impossible goal, motivation to get there will be lacking – It doesn't matter what I do, as I'm not going to get close anyway.

Another reason not to aim too high is that, especially when starting out something new, aiming for huge results means that you'll probably end up doing nothing. It's (relatively) easy to find half an hour to write a draft for a simple blog post and another hour or two two actually write it out, and maybe 45 more minutes to fix the mistakes and go through improvement suggestions that you got from your friends / co-workers (Yes, always ask for feedback).

So there, a few notes on good Key Results. More will probably follow, or maybe not.
